NCC

National Cadet Corps (NCC), came into existence on 15 July 1948 under an Act of Parliament. Over the years, NCC has spread its activities and values, across the length and breadth of the country; in schools and colleges, in almost all the districts of India.

It has attracted millions of young boys and girls, to the very ethos espoused by its motto, “unity and discipline” and molded them into disciplined and responsible citizens of the country. NCC has attained an enviable brand value for itself, in the Young India’s mind space.

National Cadet Corps (NCC), aims at character building and leadership, in all walks of life and promotes the spirit of patriotism and National Integration amongst the youth of the country. Towards this end, it runs a multifaceted training; varied in content, style and processes, with added emphasis on practical training, outdoor training and training as a community.

Bharatiya Vidya Bhavan has two NCC battalions namely 3 Karnataka and 13 Karnataka with an annual enrollment of 50 cadets each.

3 Kar Battalion is exclusively for girls.

Bhajan

Bhajan is the generic name for any kind of Indian devotional song. Bhajan is a Sanskrit word meaning “singing to glorify God." At Bharatiya Vidya Bhavan, we teach Bhajans to children as a way of introducing them to and building their interest in music. They are an integral part of the school’s culture and are part of the student’s timetable.

The serenity of the lyrics, the tender rendering of a soulful note, the positive energy of a group singing in harmony, or the absorbed yearning in an individual voice refresh the minds of young and result in higher performance.

Abacus

Abacus learning is one of the best ways to improve arithmetic skills. Abacus math also helps develop an interest and passion in math for many students as their confidence and understanding grows from an early age. In our school, students are introduced to Abacus from class 1. They use the abacus in class till class 4. It’s been proven that Abacus is one of the most effective methods for young learners to do mental math.

We have observed that when students learn to use an abacus, they use their hands and a tool with beads to calculate numbers. It is proven that the movement of fingers gives them an opportunity to activate the brain sensors. The activity in the right hand helps them to coordinate and the left side of the brain and vice versa. This brings several benefits to the brain such as:

  • A stress-free way of learning math concepts which build confidence
  • Calculation skills are enhanced and students can complete calculations faster and accurately
  • It helps with logical reasoning and enhances problem-solving abilities
  • Abacus programs not only teach math but also help in improving concentration levels, boosts self-confidence and self-esteem
  • It strengthens mental skills like visualization
Sports

The school has a playground measuring 1.25 acres with facilities for handball, volleyball and basketball courts. Sports develop the physical as well as mental strength in students. Daily physical exercise is essential for students because exercise not only helps students to stay healthy, but it also helps to improve their emotional fitness.

Sports is a major part of the school curriculum and it helps them to stay emotionally and physically healthy. Participation in school sports increases confidence, mental alertness and self-esteem. It helps to create various skills such as leadership, patience, team work and social skills.

Yoga

In today’s fast-paced and technology-driven world, the physical and mental well-being of school students is more important than ever. With increasing academic pressures and a sedentary lifestyle, students often struggle with stress, anxiety, and lack of focus. It is in this context that the ancient practice of yoga holds immense significance.

Yoga not only promotes physical fitness but also cultivates emotional stability, mental clarity, and overall holistic development. It provides students

  • Physical fitness and flexibility
  • Emotional well being
  • Reduced stress
  • Better concentration and academic performance
  • Cultivates a sense of discipline
  • Develops social skills

Field Trips

Bharatiya vidya bhavan recognises the myriad benefits of outdoor education especially field trips. Venturing beyond the confines of school walls, students step into a world of experiential learning, where real-world experiences become powerful educational tools.

Different types of field trips such as educational, nature, industrial and adventure trips are planned throughout the year. Field trips make learning more engaging, memorable, and relevant. They promote critical thinking, enhance observational skills, and nurture a sense of curiosity and wonder.

House Activities

Year-long house activities are planned and shared in the annual calendar of events. Students get an opportunity to participate in activities such as Rangoli making, Mandala art, Mehandi, Ikebana, cooking without fire,story telling, creating wealth out of waste, origami, storytelling and so on.

The activities are spread across from kindergarten to class 10. Age-appropriate and innovative activities are planned throughout the year and phased out to creatively engage students in co-curricular activities.
